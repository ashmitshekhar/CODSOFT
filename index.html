Late Shri Ratan Tata

December 28, 1937 - October 9, 2024

Industrialist and Philanthropist

If you want to walk fast, walk alone. But if you want to walk far, walk together.

- Ratan Tata

Life Journey

  • 1937: Birth details – Born in Bombay (now Mumbai), India on December 28, 1937.
  • 1940s–50s: Early education/childhood milestone – Studied at Campion School in Mumbai, followed by Cathedral and John Connon School, and then moved to the U.S. to attend Riverdale Country School in New York.
  • 1962: Career beginning – Joined the Tata Group after graduating with a degree in architecture from Cornell University.
  • 1991: Major life event – Became Chairman of Tata Group, succeeding J.R.D. Tata.
  • 2008: Notable achievement – Instrumental in the launch of the Tata Nano, the world’s cheapest car, and acquisition of Jaguar-Land Rover.
  • 2012: Significant contribution – Retired as Chairman of Tata Group but continued to contribute as Chairman Emeritus; played a major role in philanthropic ventures through Tata Trusts.
  • 2023: Most recent significant event – Honored with the Order of Australia for his contributions to India-Australia business relations and philanthropy.

Major Achievements

  • Led the acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover by Tata Motors in 2008, transforming the global image of Indian industry.
  • Launched the Tata Nano, the world’s most affordable car, aimed at providing mobility for all.
  • Expanded Tata Group's global footprint with successful acquisitions like Tetley, Corus, and Jaguar Land Rover.
  • Oversaw the growth of Tata Group’s revenue more than 40 times and profit over 50 times during his leadership.
  • Honored with Padma Bhushan (2000) and Padma Vibhushan (2008), two of India's highest civilian awards.
